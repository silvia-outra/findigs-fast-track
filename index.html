Re-screens — what do I do if my prospect intends to move in prior to their estimated move-in date?

Use the "Rescreen Information Change" support ticket to notify Findigs the resident is moving in sooner than the estimated move-in date. This will ensure Findigs catches the rescreen before the move-in occurs.

Also, remember to check the resident's estimated move-in date to ensure a proper 45-day rescreen.

What do I do if my applicant is disputing a decline for ID or fraud?

If an applicant wants to dispute a declined decision that results from a fraud or ID issue, guide them to email disputeresolution@Findigs.com. The adverse action letter will say "misrepresentation of application information."

Important: As tough as it may be when an applicant you're "rooting" for is denied for fraud, all fraud decisions are based on Findigs' screening process. McKinley will not dispute or override any fraud or ID verification-related declines. We will leave fraud detection and identity verification to the professionals at Findigs.

How do I help my applicants apply together?

How do prospects apply together as a group?

Pay close attention — this is a common issue that may slow down your prospect's approval process. Guiding your prospect on the right path will get you to the finish line more quickly.

To reduce merge requests, follow these steps for inviting additional applicants:

  • Send the unique application link to only one applicant.
  • If sending them to the website, inform them to have only one person create the app and then invite the other applicants.
  • Before the application has been submitted, have applicants follow these steps to invite their additional applicants:
  • 1
    Applicant invitation — the applicant will first complete their sign up and then start their application. They will not submit the application yet.
  • 2
    Navigate to the Your Household page on their application's home page and click Start.
  • 3
    Select the number of additional applicants and click Continue.
  • 4
    Fill in the information for the additional applicants they'd like to add.
  • 5
    The invited applicant will receive their invitation email and will complete their application in Findigs, already linked to the existing application.

Merging applications — what happens if my applicants apply separately?

Best practice is to ensure everyone applies together from the start. However, when a merge needs to be processed, it's important to use specific names and links when submitting a ticket for merging. If the request is not clear, it could result in merging to the incorrect group link.

Inviting applicants after submission — please remember to submit a ticket to invite additional applicants to an application after it's already been submitted. Avoid sending the invitation through the application yourself.

If you merge an application after it is approved, you'll kick the application to "Pending Review" and the application will need to be unassigned from the Unit Status. Best practice is to ensure your applicant has all co-applicants merged before the application is decided to avoid further delay in assigning an apartment.

What do I do if the property I am transferring from has an app fee special?

Due to Fair Housing, we will ensure that any transfer that takes place will account for differences in specials such as application fees.

If one property has an application fee special (e.g., $1 app fee), and the property transferring to has standard application fees (e.g., $50 app fee), the resident would be charged the difference between the two fees (in this example, $49). This would be done through a manual quick charge to be paid by the resident on the portal.

How do I go about requesting a property transfer?

Application not yet approved

Submit a Findigs ticket using the category "Change Property / Floorplan."

Application already approved

Submit a McKinley IT ticket at itsupport@mckinley.com.

In both cases, please make sure to include the name of the property and floorplan being transferred to.

If someone reserves, then requests a transfer to another property, does the original property keep the reservation fee?

  • If the reservation is on the waitlist — McKinley will allow a transfer along with the transfer of the original reservation fee. To accommodate the transfer, please reach out to your RAM.
  • If the reservation is assigned to an actual apartment (Vacant or Notice Rented) — the future resident would lose their reservation fee. The prospect will need to provide a new reservation fee to the other McKinley property. Exceptions will apply if the reason for the transfer is due to McKinley error or out of the prospect's control such as a canceled notice (please reach out to your managers and directors in these circumstances to confirm).

What to know about underwriting process and policy

Criminal review — it's taking forever. What's the deal?

These cannot be escalated. Though we previously may have shared estimated report return times, we are not going to share those moving forward as the estimates are extremely variable. The last thing we want is to set the wrong expectations.

Any criminal report that doesn't get returned immediately means there are records being investigated manually with the courts. This is especially true in Michigan but can happen anywhere. The court systems are not all super transparent or easy to access, and thus, sometimes records are retrieved by a "runner" system. As a result, this can take some time dependent on how fast the court responds.

We know how frustrating it can feel waiting for a criminal decision to come back. Know that a longer return time is a result of the complexities of the court retrieval system, and your Findigs team will return the information as soon as it becomes available to them. Findigs has done the research for each of McKinley's core markets — Michigan and Florida — to offer McKinley the most expedient and comprehensive resources by market to ensure accurate and timely results.

All things income verification

My prospect has income on their bank statement that isn't being counted. Can I get it added?

If your applicant receives income through cash apps like Zelle or CashApp, they'll need to declare their income in the Findigs chat box to have it counted. The more specific they are on which Zelle/CashApp payments should be included, the better.

Findigs will try to match the name of each payment to the type of work the applicant indicates. If Findigs cannot match the work description to the payments, they'll count all Zelle/CashApp transactions except for:

  • Any Zelle/CashApp payment that says "transfer" will not be counted. Only payments that show as "payment."
  • Any Zelle/CashApp payment from someone with the same name as an applicant will not be counted.
  • To avoid confusion or the application going to pending, bank linking would be best provided there are regular deposits being made. How they are paid is an important part of the application discussion and should be discussed prior to applying.
Bank statements cannot be used as "supplemental income," to ensure that we aren't counting income twice. We can't combine the qualification methods to ensure consistency in screening review.

When I manually calculate the monthly income from their paystubs or bank statements, it seems different than what Findigs calculated. Why?

The Findigs calculated income number may differ from a "back of the napkin" manual calculation from our team, and that's ok. The methodology Findigs uses to calculate income — whether using bank statements, pay stubs, or direct bank/payroll link — comes up with a daily average income based on the information provided. This offers our prospects a more balanced view on income, if for example, they were paid more in one month than prior, or vice versa.

So how does income calculation work?

Manual document uploads

If the applicant uploaded bank statements or paystub docs, Findigs will take those and calculate the average pay amount per "day," and then multiply that by 30. This standardized calculation is used to make sure different types of income sources are treated equitably.

Bank/payroll linking

If the applicant bank or payroll links, Findigs will take all available transactions up to 90 days back, and calculate an average pay amount per "day," then multiply that by 30 to get the monthly verified income. This approach is designed to better accommodate applicants with lumpy income.

My prospect provided bank statements, but they are not being counted. Why not?

The 28-day minimum rule — check the notes in the application. It is likely the bank statement provided is less than 28 days of history (minimum needed). If the applicant's bank account hasn't been open for 29 days and they don't have a statement, we'll have to wait until they get their first statement to move forward.

My applicant only has physical paystubs or bank statements. How can I get these accepted?

First, the applicant will need to go into chat and state that they don't have any way to access digital copies and only have paper documents available.

Submit a support ticket to Findigs — "PM-Uploaded Documents." This ticket will allow you to explain to Findigs that the applicant is in the office and McKinley has scanned the document for the applicant.

What do I say to my prospects about using other forms of income to qualify (liquid assets, 401K, stock accounts, etc.)?

  • Findigs can only use one method of qualification for income. Findigs cannot use a combination of liquid assets and regular income for qualifications.
  • Only liquid assets can be used for income qualifications. If there is an investment account, Findigs can only use the available cash balance.

How does the offer letter verification process work?

  • Findigs will first attempt to verify the offer letter by emailing the supervisor listed on the offer letter.
    • The domain address of the email (e.g., @mckinley.com) must match the employer's website.
    • Findigs sees a high incidence of fraud when the domains don't match.
  • If email verification isn't possible, the process may take longer: If there is no available email on the offer letter, manual verification will be required. Findigs will call via the phone number available through business lookup. They will not accept any number provided by the applicant.
My applicant just opened a bank account. What is the best way to verify their income? Instruct the applicant to choose bank linking in this case.

How does Findigs' Identity Verification (IDV) and Synthetic Fraud Detection work?

Findigs has seen a rise in applicants supplying fake Social Security Numbers to hide previous evictions or negative credit history. Findigs' Synthetic Fraud Detection is designed to catch when applicants are using fraudulent SSNs when another SSN is a stronger match to that individual, indicating they are not providing the correct ID information.

99.8% accuracy rate on catching fraudulent Social Security Numbers in Findigs' initial tests.
  • Detected immediately upon app submission. Many of the document fraud cases we've seen also have used a fake SSN. This is a major addition as it allows us to detect these immediately and save time from following up getting income documents from applicants who will not qualify.
  • Those with no SSN or using passport numbers will not be affected. This is very important as we are not looking to negatively affect our large immigrant/international resident population.

If an applicant wants to dispute a declined decision that results from a fraud or ID issue, guide them to email disputeresolution@Findigs.com. The adverse action letter emailed by Findigs directly to the applicant will say "misrepresentation of application information."

Tips to ensure your application is decisioned quickly.
